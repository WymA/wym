POSTULATE

The enunciation of a self-evident problem, in distinction froman axiom, which is the enunciation of a self-evident theorem.The distinction between a postulate and an axiom lies in this, --that the latter is admitted to be self-evident, while the former maybe agreed upon between two reasoners, and admitted by both, but notas proposition which it would be impossible to deny. Eng. Cyc.
