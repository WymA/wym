PAWL

PAWL

A pivoted tongue, or sliding bolt, on one part of a machine,adapted to fall into notches, or interdental spaces, on another part,as a ratchet wheel, in such a manner as to permit motion in onedirection and prevent it in the reverse, as in a windlass; a catch,click, or detent. See Illust. of Ratchet Wheel. [Written also paul,or pall.] Pawl bitt (Naut.), a heavy timber, set abaft the windlass,to receive the strain of the pawls.-- Pawl rim or ring (Naut.), a stationary metallic ring surroundingthe base of a capstan, having notches for the pawls to catch in.
