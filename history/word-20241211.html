Econ.), the aggregate capital existing at any timein any country, which theoretically is unconditionally destined to bepaid out in wages. It was formerly held, by Mill and other politicaleconomists, that the average rate of wages in any country at any timedepended upon the relation of the wages fund to the number oflaborers.
