EARTHQUAKE

A shaking, trembling, or concussion of the earth, due tosubterranean causes, often accompanied by a rumbling noise. The waveof shock sometimes traverses half a hemisphere, destroying cities andmany thousand lives; -- called also earthdin, earthquave, andearthshock. Earthquake alarm, a bell signal constructed to operate onthe theory that a few seconds before the occurrence of an earthquakethe magnet temporarily loses its power.
