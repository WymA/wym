OTTO CYCLE

OTTO CYCLE

A four-stroke cycle for internal-combustion engines consistingof the following operations: First stroke, suction into cylinder ofexplosive charge, as of gas and air; second stroke, compression,ignition, and explosion of this charge; third stroke (the workingstroke), expansion of the gases; fourth stroke, expulsion of theproducts of combustion from the cylinder. This is the cycle inventedby Beau de Rochas in 1862 and applied by Dr. Otto in 1877 in theOtto-Crossley gas engine, the first commercially successful internal-combustion engine made.
